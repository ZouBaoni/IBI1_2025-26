This practical about infectious disease modelling helped me understand how to translate real-world problems into computer simulations. 
I learned the basic logic of the SIR model, including how susceptible people become infected and how infected people recover. 
I also understood how vaccination can reduce the spread of disease and achieve herd immunity.

The spatial SIR model was the most challenging part, 
especially simulating the spread between neighbouring cells in a 2D grid. 
I encountered some errors, such as negative population numbers and plotting problems, 
but I fixed them by adding condition checks and improving the code structure.

Through this practical, I became more familiar with numpy and matplotlib, 
and I realised how computer programming can be used to simulate biological and medical problems. 
This was a very meaningful and useful programming exercise.
